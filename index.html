About Me

I am a Computer Science undergraduate (B.Tech 2024-2028) at SRMIST.

My work centers on the end-to-end machine learning pipeline, from rigorous data preprocessing to advanced model training and, crucially, model interpretability using methods like SHAP. I am driven by the challenge of building AI systems that are not only powerful but also transparent, fair, and reliable enough for high-stakes applications.

Currently, I am deeply engaged in research that bridges theoretical foundations with practical impact—such as modeling dopamine-triggering patterns in digital media using interpretable ML. My goal is to contribute to AI systems that enhance human well-being while rigorously addressing ethical and safety concerns. In parallel, I actively build scalable, reproducible software tools and open datasets to push forward the frontier of transparent AI, preparing myself to excel at top-tier graduate programs and research labs globally.
